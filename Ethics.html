Privacy has been for a long time and still is a big dilema when using technology. As quoted from a reseach paper focusing on privacy and information technology by standford encyclopedia of philosophy archive, "Recent advances in information technology threaten privacy and have reduced the amount of control over personal data and open up the possibility of a range of negative consequences as a result of access to personal data (Van den Hoven, 2019). It is clear that in a world of technology where information is transfered from place to place containg personal information or not, privacy should be kept private and only accesible to those its meant for. Privacy is just one example of many ethical issues that arises in technology. To continue our disuccion on ethics, I will address how can we improve the ethical problems we face in information technlogy.


The most important solution to balance ethical values and developing new technologies is to find and create a set of guidelines or rules that are established to form a grouded idea on what is considered ethcial or not in terms of technology and its use in society. An emerging research topic that grasps this idea is robot ethics. According to the Association for Advacning automation, an assocaiton who focuses on the automation of everything technology, "Robot ethics combines insights from experts in robotics, AI, computer science, and engineering with insights from experts in philosophy, law, psychology and sociology, in an attempt to ensure that automation designs and deployments do not create ethical hazards for individuals and society" (A3, 2022). As stated above combining the insights from many ethical perspectives allows us to have a clear guideline in the design and implementation of technology in society to ensure that it is safe for society. I belive this could be a solution to combat the ongoing ethical problems seen in information technology.
